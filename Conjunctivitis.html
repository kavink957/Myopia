Conjunctivitis

Introduction

Conjunctivitis, commonly referred to as pink eye, is an inflammation of the conjunctiva, the thin, transparent membrane that covers the white part of the eye and lines the inner surface of the eyelids. It is a highly contagious condition that can be caused by various factors, including infections, allergies, or irritants. Conjunctivitis can affect people of all ages and is characterized by redness, itching, and discharge from the eyes.
